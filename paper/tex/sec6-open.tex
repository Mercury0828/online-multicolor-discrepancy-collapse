\section{The growing-$k$ regime}\label{sec:open}

\Cref{cor:bracket} leaves a gap for growing $k$. We record what the gap reduces to and why two natural approaches do not
close it; this maps the open problem precisely.

\paragraph{A sufficient lemma.}
Recall the rank $\rank$ of \eqref{eq:rank}: a range increase (``raise'') changes a coordinate by $\Delta\rank=k-\maxmult$, and
a within-level broadening (a color one below the maximum is incremented, enlarging the argmax set) changes it by
$\Delta\rank=+1$ without changing the range. By \eqref{eq:rank}, an online rule keeping $\max_{t,i}\rank_i(t)\le C(k\,\Psifn+\Hscale)$
would give $\ondisc_k=\Th(\max\{\Psifn,\loglog n/k\})$, closing the bracket. The aggregate level-raising cover bound (the
analogue used in the offline analysis) controls raises; the missing piece is control of the within-level broadenings.

\paragraph{Two obstructions.}
The natural symmetric approach, a moment hierarchy in $(\rangeop,\maxmult)$ alone, cannot reach the target. The broadening
cost is controlled by an adaptive estimate (\Cref{lem:bc}), but combined with the raise cover it yields only a $k$-fold
range recurrence $\eta_{r+1}\lesssim(C\eta_r)^k$, hence $\eta\le e^{-ck^{j}}$, so a union over $n$ coordinates needs
$j\gtrsim\loglog n/\log k$ rather than the $\loglog n/k$ the bracket allows: a $\log k$ gap. A deterministic ``skip''
configuration (one coordinate per color at profile $(r,0,\dots,0)$) shows that no permutation-invariant rule in
$(\rangeop,\maxmult)$ avoids the $k^{r}$ branching, so the symmetric route is genuinely obstructed (this is an obstruction to
a proof method, not an online lower bound). An asymmetric, color-sensitive rule recovers the right ``staggered Fibonacci''
branching $F_k(m)=\sum_{j=1}^{k}F_k(m-j)=\Th(\phi_k^{m})$ with $\phi_k\uparrow2$ (the mechanism behind the $\loglog n/k$ load
of \emph{Always-Go-Left} allocation \cite{BCSV2006}); but the expectation-level tail it would need is false, refuted by an
explicit ``phase conveyor'' (\Cref{app:bc}) that recycles one coordinate to produce consecutive phases with linearly many
arrivals. That construction has probability far below $n^{-A}$, so it refutes the expectation bound and the quenched
factorization, not the high-probability statement.

\begin{problem}\label{prob:growing}
Determine $\ondisc_k$ for growing $k$ ($k\le d$, $d,k=(\log n)^{o(1)}$). We conjecture
$\ondisc_k=\Th(\max\{\Psifn(\lam,\log k),\ \loglog n/k\})$. By \Cref{app:bc}, this follows from a truncated, cycle-aware
high-probability bound on the asymmetric rule's phase births.
\end{problem}
