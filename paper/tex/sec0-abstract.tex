We study how the number of colors affects discrepancy in the average-case online vector-balancing model of Altschuler and
Tikhomirov, where i.i.d.\ uniform sparse $0/1$ vectors are colored to keep every color load balanced at every coordinate
and every prefix. Altschuler and Tikhomirov determined the two-color online value; we determine the offline value for a
growing number of colors and the online value for every fixed number, and reduce the remaining growing-palette online case
to a single high-probability lemma.

Offline, more colors help: in the growing-palette regime the optimal discrepancy of a $k$-coloring drops from its two-color
value to a balls-into-bins occupancy threshold, which we pin down up to constants and match by an explicit coloring built
from a negative-association structural estimate, a density-sensitive partial-coloring step, and a recursive color tree.
Online, more colors do not change the order for any fixed number of them: the doubly-logarithmic barrier of the two-color
case persists, by an extension of the seed-and-repair scheme to many colors. Together these give a multicolor
online--offline separation. For a growing number of colors the online value is bracketed by the two; we identify a sufficient
high-probability lemma about an asymmetric online rule, and identify a concrete obstruction showing that a natural symmetric
moment hierarchy incurs a $\log k$ loss.
