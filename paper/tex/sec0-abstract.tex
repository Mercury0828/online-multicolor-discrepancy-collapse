We study how the number of colors affects discrepancy in the average-case online vector-balancing model of Altschuler and
Tikhomirov, where i.i.d.\ uniform sparse $0/1$ vectors are colored to keep every color load balanced at every coordinate
and every prefix. Altschuler and Tikhomirov determined the two-color online value; we determine the multicolor value in two
of the three regimes of the color count and reduce the third to a single high-probability lemma.

Offline, more colors help: the optimal discrepancy of a $k$-coloring drops from its two-color value to a balls-into-bins
occupancy threshold, which we identify exactly and match by an explicit coloring built from a negative-association
structural estimate, a density-sensitive partial-coloring step, and a recursive color tree. Online, more colors do not help
for any fixed number of them: the doubly-logarithmic barrier of the two-color case persists, by an extension of the
seed-and-repair scheme to many colors. Together these give a multicolor online--offline separation. For a growing number of
colors the online value is bracketed by the two, and we show the exact order reduces to one lemma about an asymmetric online
rule, while the symmetric approach provably cannot reach it.
